Top Music Artists

Best Selling Music Artists of All Time
Final Project by Emilia Kunst

The aim of this final project is to visualize interesting aspects about the Best selling Music Artists of All Time data set, that condiders all music artists from the 20th century to the present who breached the mark of 75 milions claimed record sales worldwide. Goal is to show the most influential aspects of the music industry. The header of this webpage gives an overview of who is in that list of artists and the font size is relative to their claimed sales. Try refreshing the page! It may help you to spot an artist you haven't spot before...

The Origin of Our Music

That many childhood dreams of becoming a music artist are closely associated with the wish of going overseas is no coincidence! Indeed, the sum off all the total certified units (short: TCU) that were created by American artists is an incredible total of roughly 8241.8 millions. That is nearly two thirds of the world total being 13155.8 million TCU!

This interactive world map shows where the top artists originate. The darker the grey the higher the number of TCU in that country! Hovering over a country will show both the name of the country as well as the number of TCU. Interesting is, that size appearantly really doesn't matter. Trinidad and Tobago for instance has more than half as many TCUs as Australia. Can you find it? Zooming in on your sreen might help.

Active artists - When and Where?

What clearly matters is the number of artists that come from a country as the following lineplot shows! It is obvious that the top 3 in terms of TCU (US, UK and Canada) also dominate here. There were times when America had more than 70 of the best selling music artists active at the same time! Ever since 1975 the United Kingdom is pretty constant between 10 and 20 active artists. And in the last 10 years also Canada stays constant with around 5 active artists.

This interactive lineplot shows in which year how many of the top artists were active from each country. Hovering over the legend will focus that exact timeline, which helps understanding the development in the countries with a lower number of artists.

The longer the career, the bigger the success?

Interestingly, it doesn't seem to work like that. Clearly there are artists whose persitence payed off like Elton John with a career of over 55 years. But Drake who is the artist with the most TCU has been active for only around 20 years. And The Beatles had a career of approximately 10 years and are still the artist with the highest amount of claimed sales!

This interactive bubblechart shows how the duration of these artists' careers correlates to the amount of TCU they created (on the Y-axis) and the total amount of sales they reached (the size of the bubbles). Hovering over the countries in the legend will focus only on the artists from that particular country. Hovering over the bubbles will show the name of the artist the bubble represents.

Male Domination in The Music Industry

Like most domains also the music industry seems to be male-dominated. Only in 1960 the first carreers of female top muisc artists started. Until now the percentage of women in the yearly active top muisc artists is under a third!

This interactive barplot shows the distriburion of gender over the years among the top music artists. In case of bands the main artist/lead singer is used to determine the gender. Noteworthy is also that this binary depiction of gender is not meant to exclude any person. There are simply no artists in the dataset that indentify as non-binary. Hovering over the legend will focus on the number of that gender only. Hovering over the bars shows the exact number of active artists of that gender in that year.

The Music That We Like

But what music is successful? What music do most people prefer to listen to? Clearly the name Pop(ular) is well reasoned as it is the genre with the highest sum of TCU that were produced in that genre. But also Hip-Hop, R&B and Rock are very much influential genres.
